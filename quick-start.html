Quick Start

start where it hurts.

We work with startups, small businesses, product teams, and the experimental edges of larger organizations. 02 / The first move

We start where the noise is loudest.

The shape is roughly four weeks. We spend the first one in discovery, build a focused piece of work and get it live across the next two, and use the fourth to measure what happened and capture what we learned. By the end you have something visible and measurable in the room, and both of us have an honest read on whether continuing makes sense, which is the whole point of beginning this way. Quick Start is designed so that the work itself proves the fit before either side commits to a longer engagement.

03 / What comes after Quick Start

When the first work lands, the longer engagement follows.

We stay close to your team as the scope grows, and as confidence builds on both sides the different pieces of work begin to reinforce one another rather than competing for the same attention. Here is what tends to build out from there.

  1. 01 Marketing system A marketing engine that feeds the funnel, with the campaigns and stories the people you want to reach actually respond to.
  2. 02 Sales funnel with CRM A CRM your team will use because it's shaped around how they actually sell, with thoughtful AI automation in the places it saves real hours.
  3. 03 Brand and content Brand and writing that catch up to who you've become, in a voice the people you want to reach will recognize as yours.
  4. 04 Social, run as presence A steady presence in the channels that matter to your audiences, with social listening woven into how the work runs day to day.
  5. 05 Paid acquisition Paid campaigns that build on each other, so what you spend in one month carries momentum into the next.
  6. 06 Community that holds A community with reasons to stay close between launches, so you're not starting from zero every time you have something to say.
04 / Why this holds up

What you pay for is what the work produces.

Every engagement runs through our two frameworks. The Communications Compass keeps us honest about who you're actually trying to reach, and the Way of Work shapes how we get there together. As the work moves forward it measures itself, the learning compounds across episodes, and your team stays close enough to the process that when we eventually step back you can carry it forward on your own.
